MRI Scan

A magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scan is a painless test that uses radio waves and strong magnetic fields to produce clear images of the inside of the body. MRIs can create detailed images of nearly every structure and organ inside the body.

X-ray

X-rays are a form of electromagnetic radiation, similar to visible light. Unlike light, however, x-rays have higher energy and can pass through most objects, including the body. Medical x-rays are used to generate images of tissues and structures inside the body.

CT Scan

A computed tomography (CT) scan is a diagnostic imaging procedure that uses X-rays and computer technology to create images of the body. CT scans can be used to identify injuries or diseases in various parts of the body.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs either when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or when the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces. Insulin is a hormone that regulates blood glucose.
